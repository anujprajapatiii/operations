## The Protocol

### Morning Sunlight (7:50–8:10 AM)

From [[THE PERFECT DAY]]:
- Go to the terrace. 15-20 minutes.
- Face toward the sky (not directly at the sun).
- Do a 30-second dead hang or pull-up hold while you're up there.
- No phone. Just you and the sun.

**Why this timing matters:** Morning light between 7-9 AM is the strongest signal to your suprachiasmatic nucleus to set your circadian clock. It tells your body "this is daytime" and begins the 14-16 hour countdown to melatonin release.

### Afternoon Sunlight (4:30–5:00 PM)

From [[THE PERFECT DAY]]:
- Go to the terrace/loft. 20-30 minutes.
- No scrolling. Walk around, breathe, look at the sky.
- This regulates your circadian rhythm and gives you a second wind.

**Why this matters:** Late afternoon light exposure signals your body to prepare for the transition toward evening. It anchors the end of the "active" phase.

## Evening Light Discipline (9:00 PM onward)

- Phone on Night Shift / warm tone
- No new stressful information, no work emails, no Slack
- Soft light in the room only
- Consider the red light shades you flagged in [[Todo Back Home]] — a red/amber desk lamp for evening use cuts blue light without needing glasses

### Red Light Setup (from your todo)

- [ ] Get round red shades light for evening use
- Place in bedroom or living area for post-9 PM ambient light
- This replaces overhead lighting and signals melatonin production

## Sleep Protocol (11:00–11:30 PM)

From [[THE PERFECT DAY]]:
- Foam earplugs in
- Blackout the room completely
- HEPA air filter on
- Cool the room (AC if hot — if you can't sleep, you're probably too warm)
- Phone face down or in another room

**Target:** Lights out by 11:30 PM. This gives you 7.5 hours to a 7:00 AM wake-up — five full 90-minute sleep cycles.
