## The Gap

From [[Future Ready Designer 2026]]: production-level design is being automated. Your visual/motion skills are strong but the market is shifting. The premium is now on: business thinking, problem framing, facilitation, and AI tool fluency.

From [[Feedback Note]]: your own work feedback says to stop jumping into tickets without the right questions, reframe tickets as product problems, and get alignment early.

This isn't about courses for their own sake. It's about closing specific gaps that show up in your daily work and your next job interviews.

---

## Skill 1: Problem Framing (Highest Priority)

From [[Future Ready Designer 2026]]: *"The bottleneck is increasingly not 'how do we design this?' but 'what should we even build?'"*

**6-month concrete action** (from the same note): Pick one complex, ambiguous project at work — volunteer to lead the framing phase. Document your process. That artifact and experience is worth more than any course.

### Practice at BrowserStack
- [ ] Identify the next ambiguous project in Growth pod
- [ ] Volunteer to lead the problem-framing phase
- [ ] Use the questioning framework from [[Feedback Note]]:
  - Who is this for?
  - What's the current behavior?
  - Why now?
  - What metrics will move?
  - What's the baseline?
  - What's the hypothesis?
  - What are the guardrails?
- [ ] Document the framing process — this becomes a portfolio artifact

### Self-Study
- [ ] Read "Good Strategy Bad Strategy" by Richard Rumelt (strategy framing)
- [ ] Study 3 published design case studies that emphasize framing over execution
- [ ] Practice: take any BrowserStack ticket and write a 1-paragraph problem statement before touching Figma

## The ZEST Self-Check (from [[Road to Lead Visual Designer]])

Weekly, ask yourself:
- **Zing:** Did I bring energy and direction this week?
- **Empathy:** Do I understand where each person is at?
- **Stretch:** Did I create space for someone to grow?
- **Taste:** Did I raise the bar on quality this week?
