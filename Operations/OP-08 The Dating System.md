## The Philosophy

From [[What I am looking in a partner]]: you want someone in a creative field, health-conscious, with classical values, who wants to build a life and family together. That's specific enough to filter, broad enough to find.

From [[Book Club Strat]]: *"Be relaxed and social with everyone, not just attractive women... Let conversations happen naturally."*

This isn't a pickup system. It's about consistently being in the right rooms, looking and feeling good, and being genuinely interested in people. The system handles the logistics so you can focus on being present.

---

## The Funnel

Think of it like a design problem (because you're a designer):

```
Visibility   →  You exist and look good (profiles, social media, presence)
Proximity    →  You're in rooms with people (events, classes, cafes)
Connection   →  You talk to people with genuine curiosity
Chemistry    →  Something clicks — exchange contact, make a plan
Date         →  Coffee, walk, activity — low pressure, real conversation
Relationship →  Multiple good dates, mutual interest, intentional decision
```

Your bottleneck right now is between Visibility and Proximity. The operations above fix that. This operation handles Connection → Date.

## Channel Strategy

### Real World (Primary — 70% of effort)
- Meetups, events, book clubs, martial arts, coffee shops
- Friends of friends introductions
- Work and industry events
- Ask friends to set you up — explicitly tell people you're looking

### Dating Apps (Secondary — 20% of effort)
- 10 minutes per day maximum. Swipe intentionally.
- When you match: move to a real conversation fast (within 3-4 messages)
- Suggest a specific plan: "Want to grab coffee at [place] on [day]?"
- Don't let conversations stall in-app — either meet or move on

### Shaadi / Family Network (Parallel — 10% of effort)
- Biodata ready to share
- Family members briefed on what you're looking for
- Respond to introductions within 24 hours
- Take every introduction seriously — even if just one coffee

## The Date Playbook

### First Date
- **Format:** Coffee or a walk. 60-90 minutes. Low investment, easy exit for both.
- **Location:** Your regular coffee spot or a place you genuinely like
- **Prep:** Be clean, dress well (not overdressed), smell good
- **Mindset:** You're finding out if you like them, not performing for approval
- **Questions that work:** What are you working on? What do you do when you're not working? What's something you changed your mind about recently?
- **After:** Text within 2 hours if you had a good time. Be direct.

### Second Date
- **Format:** Activity together. Cooking, a walk in a new neighbourhood, an exhibition, a class.
- **Why:** Shared experience reveals more than conversation across a table.
- **Timeline:** Within 1 week of first date. Don't let momentum die.

### Third Date+
- You'll know by now. If it's good, keep going. If it's not, be honest and kind.

## Tracking (Keep It Simple)

In your Sunday weekly review, answer these:
1. How many new people did I meet this week?
2. Did I go on a date this week?
3. Did I follow up with everyone I said I would?
4. What worked? What didn't?
5. What's the plan for next week?

Don't overthink metrics. The leading indicator is: *am I consistently in rooms with people?*

## The Emergency Social Protocol

If you feel yourself withdrawing:
1. Text one friend right now. Anything. Even "what are you up to this weekend?"
2. Go to your regular coffee spot. Order something. Sit there for 30 minutes.
3. If there's an event this week, commit to going. Tell someone you're going so you're accountable.
4. Remember: *"Everyone is shy. Other people are waiting for you to introduce yourself."*
