# OP-11: Money & Financial Discipline

> **Parent:** [[Career & Money Overhaul]]
> **Status:** Not started
> **Goal:** Know your numbers, control your spending, protect your runway

---

## The Situation

From [[2026 Strategy]]: *"Money gets spent fast. We need to optimise and operate at a tight margin and on credit."*

You're planning a job transition within 6 months. That means you need to know exactly how much runway you have, cut unnecessary spending, and build a buffer. This isn't about becoming frugal — it's about having the financial clarity to make career moves without panic.

---

## Phase 1: Financial Audit (This Week)

Before you can optimise, you need to see clearly.

- [ ] Export last 3 months of bank/credit card statements
- [ ] Categorise spending: Rent, Food, Transport, Subscriptions, Social, Health, Misc
- [ ] Calculate monthly burn rate (average of last 3 months)
- [ ] Calculate current savings / runway (savings ÷ monthly burn = months of runway)
- [ ] Identify the top 3 spending categories eating your money

---

## Phase 2: Cut & Optimise (Week 2)

### Subscriptions Audit
From [[2026 Strategy]] — file-over-app, move to open source:

- [ ] List every active subscription (apps, services, streaming, etc.)
- [ ] Cancel anything you haven't used in 30 days
- [ ] Move to free/open-source alternatives where possible:
  - Notion → Obsidian (already done)
  - 1Password → consider Bitwarden or KeePass
  - Paid tools → free tiers or open alternatives
- [ ] Set calendar reminders for free trial expirations

### Food Spending
Your nutrition operation ([[OP-01 Nutrition Reboot]]) handles this — cooking at home saves significant money compared to Swiggy/eating out. Track the difference.

### Transport
- Driving lessons are an investment (from [[2026 Strategy]])
- But optimise: combine trips, use public transport when sensible

## Phase 3: Monthly Financial Rhythm (Ongoing)

### Thursday Financial Check-In (from [[Perfect Week Index]])

Every Thursday, 15 minutes:
- [ ] Check account balances
- [ ] Review this week's spending — any surprises?
- [ ] Are you on track for the month's budget?
- [ ] Any upcoming large expenses?

### Monthly Review (Last Sunday of each month)
- [ ] Total spending this month by category
- [ ] Compare to last month — trending up or down?
- [ ] Update runway calculation
- [ ] Set next month's budget targets
- [ ] Review: did cooking at home save money? How much?

---

## Phase 4: Build Runway (April–August)

You need runway for the job transition — enough savings to be comfortable saying no to a bad offer.

**Target:** 3 months of expenses saved before making any job move.

### How to Build It
1. Set a fixed savings amount per month (even small — consistency matters)
2. Any freelance or side income goes straight to savings
3. Reduce discretionary spending by 20% (social, entertainment, impulse purchases)
4. Negotiate salary at new job from a position of having options, not desperation

---

## Budget Framework (Simple)

| Category | Monthly Target | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Rent | Fixed | Non-negotiable |
| Groceries | Moderate | Cooking at home = much cheaper than Swiggy |
| Transport | Low | Public transport + occasional driving lessons |
| Subscriptions | Minimal | After audit — cut to essentials |
| Social / Dating | Moderate | Coffee dates and events — not expensive if intentional |
| Health | Moderate | Supplements, gym, medical appointments |
| Savings | Fixed % | Set and forget — automate if possible |
| Buffer | 10% of income | For surprises |
